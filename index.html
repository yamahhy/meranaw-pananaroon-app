About Meranaw Pananaroon

What It Is

This platform is a digital initiative dedicated to the preservation, digitization, and revitalization of Meranaw Pananaroon (proverbs), utilizing the power of Natural Language Processing (NLP) and modern web technologies. It serves as an evolving archive that bridges cultural heritage and innovation.

Why It Was Created

Meranaw proverbs carry centuries of wisdom, values, and identity—yet many remain undocumented or inaccessible in today's digital age. Traditional preservation methods often struggle with issues of reach, sustainability, and engagement. This platform was built to address those challenges, offering a sustainable, interactive, and tech-driven solution to ensure that these oral traditions are not only safeguarded but also shared, studied, and celebrated.

Who It's For

This project is designed for:

Researchers exploring linguistics, culture, or artificial intelligence
Students and educators seeking authentic local materials
Meranaw communities aiming to reconnect with their heritage
Cultural advocates and language enthusiasts interested in Philippine indigenous knowledge

Mission

To safeguard and amplify the voice of Meranaw heritage by making traditional proverbs accessible, understandable, and relevant through cutting-edge digital tools and NLP-driven features.

Vision

A future where Meranaw Pananaroon are widely known, integrated into academic discourse, celebrated in cultural spaces, and preserved as a living tradition that contributes to the global mosaic of wisdom and identity.

Kauntol ko Singanin

Integrity on Purpose

The pananaroon in the occasions of courtship (kapamanganakan), enthronement (kandato) and family origin (kabangensa), conflict resolution (kapamasad sa rido), and self-morality (kapamangtuma) highlight the principles of Integrity on Purpose because each word and meaning demonstrates integrity on the speaker's purpose from the denotative and connotative meanings of existence, as well as the figurative language used.

Integrity on purpose (Kauntol ko singanin) is honesty while speaking. Each pananaroon has its purposes, which are the intention, occasion, situation, and impact on the listener. Some of the words show the reality of human weakness, indicating that he needs to change his behavior, while others show the person's talent, indicating the skill of the person to whom he is speaking.

Key Aspects:

  • Demonstrates family history and pride in enthronement and family origin occasions
  • Maintains harmonious interpersonal relationships through verbal eloquence
  • Uses metaphor and metonymy to imply comparison and connection
  • Emphasizes the importance of how something is said over what is said

Kaungangen ko kataruwa-on

Language Used with Wisdom

In the principle of language used with wisdom, the speaker should apply wisdom to the language he uses in the pananaroon. When a person expresses a pananaroon, he must analyze the occasion and his intention to express the pananaroon in the best way he can.

Five Essential Speech Competencies:

1. Understandable Speech

Use appropriate volume and clear articulation to ensure the message is understood.

2. Friendly & Passionate Tone

Use appropriate vocal keys and show energy and enthusiasm.

3. Balanced Speaking/Listening

Keep messages brief and focus on the listener rather than yourself.

4. Proper Speech Rate

Maintain 140-160 words per minute for optimal comprehension.

5. Eliminate Distractions

Avoid word fillers, excessive body movements, and other speech distractors.

Kasela-i ko mga Datu/Bae

Respect for Authority

Respect for authority (kasela-i ko mga datu/bae) is the main principle of all Meranaws; that is why they reflect it to the pananaroon. Meranaws believe that one factor of true Meranaw is to respect elders because if not, he/she will be considered as slave's attitude (usek).

Cultural Significance:

The Meranaw family structure, similar to Malaysian and Chinese families, places great emphasis on respecting parents, elders, and ancestors. This principle is central to moral codes and is particularly important in tribal societies.

"Nowhere have parents been more venerated by their children than among the nations of archaic culture" - Westermarck (1912)

So kipetumanen ko atastanggungan

Implementation of Responsibility

Being responsible for the Meranaws is important in their culture. Titles are used in Meranaws, so those in political or traditional positions should carry out their responsibilities without hesitation.

Leadership Collaboration:

Traditional and local political leaders must work together to achieve or promote peace in communities like Marawi City. The implementation of responsibility is like coordination with other leaders, where Sultans, Datus, and other Meranaw traditional leaders engage in functional inter and intra-community coordination within the framework of collaborative governance.

Kambilangatawi ko parangay

Modesty in Behavior

Speaking in Meranaw is a process that reveals a person's inner thoughts and movements. Speaking the pananaroon can change the behavior of an individual and improve modesty.

Kambilangataw (Modesty):

Described as the art of living Meranaw culture, imbued with Meranaw values and traditions. It embodies all the mores that describe a Meranaw, particularly the Meranaw woman, reflecting deep harmony in relationships based on status.

Reflects maratabat (honor and pride), politeness, courtesy, gentility, tact, and diplomacy

Social Etiquette Principles

Principles of Social Interaction

The social etiquette principles of pananaroon are derived from the analysis of denotative and connotative, and figurative language used, showing how Meranaw culture values proper social interaction.

Give Genuine Compliments

Kapembantuga ko salakaw di salakaw

Requires compliments to the listener or audience. Giving is a fundamental principle of good manners, focusing on appearance, abilities, and personality traits.

Language Awareness

Kapananggilay Ko Kataru

Use precise words appropriate for the occasion. Includes asking for excuses (kapanabiya) and careful analysis to avoid family feuds.

Reveal the Truth

Kipayagen ko benar

Lying is not recommended when using pananaroon. The audience expects to hear the truth, and speakers must learn etiquette when telling the truth.

Loud Articulation of Voice

Ipemaungangen so Lagam

Helps in visualizing the speaker's words. Involves proper tone, stress, and voice modulation to capture audience attention.

Sources

The proverbs featured in this system were collected from several validated and culturally significant texts, each contributing to the preservation and understanding of Meranaw wisdom and cultural heritage.

Pananadem Pananaroon ago Thoba-Thobad o Manga Lokes

by Prof. Sofailah Latip-Panggaga

A comprehensive collection of traditional Meranaw proverbs and riddles, meticulously gathered and documented by Prof. Latip-Panggaga. This foundational text serves as one of the most extensive repositories of Meranaw wisdom literature.

184 proverbs

So Manga Pananaroon Sa Ranaw: Reflections of Meranaw Culture and Worldview

by Bantog & Sarip-Macarambon

An analytical exploration of Meranaw proverbs that delves deep into their cultural significance and worldview. This scholarly work provides context and interpretation of traditional sayings within the broader framework of Meranaw society.

Montiya: Book of Meranaw Poems, Dialogues, and Peace Sayings

by Dr. Johara D. Alangca-Azis

A rich collection that encompasses various forms of Meranaw literary expression, including poems, dialogues, and peace sayings. This work highlights the artistic and philosophical dimensions of Meranaw oral tradition.

Sempad a Maradika a Paninggalan sa Ranao

by Tingcap G. Pandi and Jamel S. Macarampat

A valuable compilation of noble heritage and traditional teachings from the Ranao region. This collaborative work preserves important cultural knowledge and ancestral wisdom of the Meranaw people.

Engage with Us

Join us in preserving and enriching Meranaw cultural heritage

Contribute a Proverb

We warmly encourage individuals to take part in the preservation and enrichment of Meranaw cultural heritage by contributing traditional Pananaroon (proverbs). This initiative is open to all—whether you are a native speaker, a scholar, or someone with knowledge of Meranaw traditions, anyone may submit.

Your contribution plays a vital role in safeguarding the wisdom embedded in Meranaw oral literature. To ensure accuracy, cultural relevance, and integrity, each submitted proverb undergoes a careful review process prior to official inclusion in the collection.

Submission Requirements

To contribute a proverb, please complete the designated form by providing the following information:

Meranaw Proverb

The original text of the proverb in Meranaw.

Literal Meaning

A direct, word-for-word English translation.

English Translation

An interpretive or equivalent English version that conveys the intended message.

Theme

Select the appropriate thematic category (e.g., wisdom, family, respect, perseverance).

Source

Indicate the origin or reference of the proverb (e.g., oral tradition, community elder, written source).

Review and Verification

Upon submission, your entry will be recorded in our database and forwarded to a designated cultural expert or review panel. This team will evaluate the authenticity, clarity, and cultural appropriateness of the proverb. Once verified and approved, your contribution will be published as part of our official Pananaroon collection.

How to Access the Form

You may access the contribution form by clicking the "Contribute a Proverb" button (➕ icon) in the sidebar or the Contribute Now button below.

We deeply appreciate your participation in preserving and promoting Meranaw cultural knowledge.
